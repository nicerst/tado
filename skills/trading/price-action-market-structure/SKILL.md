# Price Action Market Structure

Read the chart in two layers: macro structure first, micro price action second.

## When to use / when NOT to use

Use when:
- The task is discretionary chart reading.
- The user wants trend, range, swings, support/resistance, or candlestick analysis.
- The setup should be based on price and structure before indicators.

Do not use when:
- The market is too choppy to label cleanly.
- The user wants pure indicator systems.
- The task is fundamental valuation, news trading, or execution automation.

## Procedure

1. Start with market structure on the higher timeframe:
   - Uptrend = higher highs + higher lows.
   - Downtrend = lower highs + lower lows.
   - Range = highs and lows holding the same broad zone.
2. Mark major swing highs and swing lows only.
3. Label structure events:
   - Break of structure = continuation in trend direction.
   - Change of character = first meaningful break against trend.
4. Mark obvious support and resistance as zones, not exact lines.
5. Read the character of the move:
   - Impulse = fast, directional, larger candles.
   - Pullback = slower, mixed candles, more wicks.
6. Drop to a lower timeframe only after the higher-timeframe bias is clear.
7. At key zones, look for price action confirmation:
   - Hammer.
   - Shooting star.
   - Bullish/bearish engulfing.
   - Momentum candle.
   - Breakout candle with strong close.
8. Reject low-quality reads:
   - Levels that are not obvious.
   - Chop with overlapping candles.
   - Signals in the middle of nowhere.

## Rules / heuristics

- Structure tells you where to care. Price action tells you when to act.
- Trends are built from impulse + pullback cycles.
- Strong levels caused real structure breaks; weak levels are more likely to get taken.
- A trendline is supporting evidence, not the reason for the trade.
- Breakouts need a clear close beyond the level, not just a wick through it.
- Pullbacks into obvious structure are better than chasing extension.
- Clean charts beat complicated charts.
- Indicators are secondary to price action and structure.

## Examples

- Uptrend -> higher low into support -> bullish engulfing -> continuation candidate.
- Range top -> shooting star at resistance -> fade candidate.
- Multi-touch resistance -> momentum close above level -> breakout candidate.
