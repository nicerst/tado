# Trading Fundamentals

Treat trading as structured decision-making under uncertainty. Risk first.

## When to use / when NOT to use

Use when:
- The user is a beginner or wants a clean refresher.
- The task is trade planning, journaling, or risk review.
- The user needs a simple framework before advanced chart work.

Do not use when:
- The task is fundamental investing or macro analysis.
- The user wants guaranteed predictions.
- The setup has no defined invalidation.

## Procedure

1. Frame trading correctly:
   - Choices are buy, sell, or do nothing.
   - Do not predict certainty. Think in probabilities.
   - Losses are business expense, not personal failure.
2. Require a valid trade structure before entry:
   - Setup.
   - Entry.
   - Stop-loss.
   - Target.
   - Position size.
3. Reject any trade missing one of those five parts.
4. Size risk small:
   - Default beginner risk: 0.5% to 1% of account per trade.
   - Wider stop = smaller size.
5. Prefer simple markets and liquid instruments:
   - Bigger stocks or major crypto first.
   - Avoid random chop and extreme volatility when learning.
6. Execute the plan without moving the stop out of emotion.
7. Review after exit:
   - Did the trade follow plan?
   - Was the stop logical?
   - Was size appropriate?
   - Was the mistake process or market variance?
8. Journal the trade:
   - Setup screenshot.
   - Entry/stop/target.
   - Result in R.
   - Notes on discipline and psychology.

## Rules / heuristics

- Most of trading is waiting.
- One trade means nothing. A sample matters.
- Win rate alone is useless. Expected value matters.
- A strategy can win less than 50% and still be profitable if winners are larger than losers.
- Never remove a stop just because price moved against you.
- FOMO and revenge trading are setup killers.
- Demo and replay practice beat impulsive live trading.
- Start simple: trend, support/resistance, price action, fixed risk.

## Examples

- Valid long plan: uptrend, pullback into support, entry at confirmation close, stop below structure, target at 2R, size = 1% account risk.
- Invalid trade: “looks bullish” with no stop, no target, and no sizing.
